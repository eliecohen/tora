["Now the LORD said unto Abram: 'Get thee out of thy country, and\n from thy kindred, and from thy father's house, unto the land that I \nwill show thee.\n","And I will make of thee a great nation, and I will bless thee, and make thy name great; and be thou a blessing.\n","And I will bless them that bless thee, and him that curseth \nthee will I curse; and in thee shall all the families of the earth be \nblessed.'\n","So Abram went, as the LORD had spoken unto him; and Lot went \nwith him; and Abram was seventy and five years old when he departed out \nof Haran.\n","And Abram took Sarai his wife, and Lot his brother's son, and \nall their substance that they had gathered, and the souls that they had \ngotten in Haran; and they went forth to go into the land of Canaan; and \ninto the land of Canaan they came.\n","And Abram passed through the land unto the place of Shechem, \nunto the terebinth of Moreh. And the Canaanite was then in the land.\n","And the LORD appeared unto Abram, and said: 'Unto thy seed will\n I give this land'; and he builded there an altar unto the LORD, who \nappeared unto him.\n","And he removed from thence unto the mountain on the east of \nBeth-el, and pitched his tent, having Beth-el on the west, and Ai on the\n east; and he builded there an altar unto the LORD, and called upon the \nname of the LORD.\n","And Abram journeyed, going on still toward the South. {P}\n","And there was a famine in the land; and Abram went down into Egypt to sojourn there; for the famine was sore in the land.\n","And it came to pass, when he was come near to enter into \nEgypt, that he said unto Sarai his wife: 'Behold now, I know that thou \nart a fair woman to look upon.\n","And it will come to pass, when the Egyptians shall see thee, \nthat they will say: This is his wife; and they will kill me, but thee \nthey will keep alive.\n","Say, I pray thee, thou art my sister; that it may be well with me for thy sake, and that my soul may live because of thee.'\n","And it came to pass, that, when Abram was come into Egypt, the Egyptians beheld the woman that she was very fair.\n","And the princes of Pharaoh saw her, and praised her to Pharaoh; and the woman was taken into Pharaoh's house.\n","And he dealt well with Abram for her sake; and he had sheep, \nand oxen, and he-asses, and men-servants, and maid-servants, and \nshe-asses, and camels.\n","And the LORD plagued Pharaoh and his house with great plagues because of Sarai Abram's wife.\n","And Pharaoh called Abram, and said: 'What is this that thou \nhast done unto me? why didst thou not tell me that she was thy wife?\n","Why saidst thou: She is my sister? so that I took her to be my wife; now therefore behold thy wife, take her, and go thy way.'\n","And Pharaoh gave men charge concerning him; and they brought him on the way, and his wife, and all that he had.\n"]
