["Now these are the ordinances which thou shalt set before them.\n","If thou buy a Hebrew servant, six years he shall serve; and in the seventh he shall go out free for nothing.\n","If he come in by himself, he shall go out by himself; if he be married, then his wife shall go out with him.\n","If his master give him a wife, and she bear him sons or \ndaughters; the wife and her children shall be her master's, and he shall\n go out by himself.\n","But if the servant shall plainly say: I love my master, my wife, and my children; I will not go out free;\n","then his master shall bring him unto God, and shall bring him \nto the door, or unto the door-post; and his master shall bore his ear \nthrough with an awl; and he shall serve him for ever. {S}\n","And if a man sell his daughter to be a maid-servant, she shall not go out as the men-servants do.\n","If she please not her master, who hath espoused her to himself,\n then shall he let her be redeemed; to sell her unto a foreign people he\n shall have no power, seeing he hath dealt deceitfully with her.\n","And if he espouse her unto his son, he shall deal with her after the manner of daughters.\n","If he take him another wife, her food, her raiment, and her conjugal rights, shall he not diminish.\n","And if he do not these three unto her, then shall she go out for nothing, without money. {S}\n","He that smiteth a man, so that he dieth, shall surely be put to death.\n","And if a man lie not in wait, but God cause it to come to hand; then I will appoint thee a place whither he may flee. {S}\n","And if a man come presumptuously upon his neighbour, to slay \nhim with guile; thou shalt take him from Mine altar, that he may die. {S}\n","And he that smiteth his father, or his mother, shall be surely put to death. {S}\n","And he that stealeth a man, and selleth him, or if he be found in his hand, he shall surely be put to death. {S}\n","And he that curseth his father or his mother, shall surely be put to death. {S}\n","And if men contend, and one smite the other with a stone, or with his fist, and he die not, but keep his bed;\n","if he rise again, and walk abroad upon his staff, then shall \nhe that smote him be quit; only he shall pay for the loss of his time, \nand shall cause him to be thoroughly healed. {S}\n","And if a man smite his bondman, or his bondwoman, with a rod, and he die under his hand, he shall surely be punished.\n","Notwithstanding if he continue a day or two, he shall not be punished; for he is his money. {S}\n","And if men strive together, and hurt a woman with child, so \nthat her fruit depart, and yet no harm follow, he shall be surely fined,\n according as the woman's husband shall lay upon him; and he shall pay \nas the judges determine.\n","But if any harm follow, then thou shalt give life for life,\n","eye for eye, tooth for tooth, hand for hand, foot for foot,\n","burning for burning, wound for wound, stripe for stripe. {S}\n","And if a man smite the eye of his bondman, or the eye of his \nbondwoman, and destroy it, he shall let him go free for his eye's sake.\n","And if he smite out his bondman's tooth, or his bondwoman's tooth, he shall let him go free for his tooth's sake. {P}\n","And if an ox gore a man or a woman, that they die, the ox \nshall be surely stoned, and its flesh shall not be eaten; but the owner \nof the ox shall be quit.\n","But if the ox was wont to gore in time past, and warning hath \nbeen given to its owner, and he hath not kept it in, but it hath killed a\n man or a woman; the ox shall be stoned, and its owner also shall be put\n to death.\n","If there be laid on him a ransom, then he shall give for the redemption of his life whatsoever is laid upon him.\n","Whether it have gored a son, or have gored a daughter, according to this judgment shall it be done unto him.\n","If the ox gore a bondman or a bondwoman, he shall give unto their master thirty shekels of silver, and the ox shall be stoned. {S}\n","And if a man shall open a pit, or if a man shall dig a pit and not cover it, and an ox or an ass fall therein,\n","the owner of the pit shall make it good; he shall give money unto the owner of them, and the dead beast shall be his. {S}\n","And if one man's ox hurt another's, so that it dieth; then \nthey shall sell the live ox, and divide the price of it;
